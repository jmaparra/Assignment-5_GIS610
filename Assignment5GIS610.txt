\section*{QCAD}\label{qcad}

\href{http://www.qcad.org/en/}{QCAD} is a free, open source application
for computer aided drafting (CAD) in two dimensions (2D). With QCAD you
can create technical drawings such as plans for buildings, interiors,
mechanical parts or schematics and diagrams. QCAD works on Windows,
macOS and Linux.

\subsection*{Reporting Bugs}\label{reporting-bugs}

Bugs can be reported and looked up at the link below:

\url{http://www.qcad.org/bugtracker/} The way in which to format the
\href{https://www.dokuwiki.org/wiki:syntax\#}{syntax} is also provided.

\subsection*{Asking Questions}\label{asking-questions}

There is a \href{https://www.qcad.org/rsforum/}{forum} for all
questions. The moderator is Andrew who is one of the developers of QCAD

\subsection*{QCAD Changelog}\label{qcad-changelog}

There are announcements for all of the bug fixes and new versions on the
forum as well a dedicated
\href{http://www.qcad.org/en/changelog}{Changelog} page indicating past
and upcoming changes.

\subsection*{Contributing}\label{contributing}

Directions on how to
\href{http://www.qcad.org/en/contribute}{contribute} are on the website
and take place on \href{https://github.com/qcad/qcad}{GitHub} by
creating a pull request.

\subsection*{Support}\label{support}

\href{https://sourceforge.net/projects/qcad/support}{SourceForge} states
that the best place to get support is on the home page and the forum for
the project. There is also a nice tutorial for using QCAD
\href{http://linuxfocus.org/English/January2002/article132.shtml\#132lfindex23}{here}.

\subsection*{Activity}\label{activity}

QCAD started as version 1 in October 1999 and has since evolved through
version 2 (September 2003) and the current version 3 (from July 2012).
There has been
\href{https://github.com/qcad/qcad/graphs/contributors}{activity} on the
sourcecode since its last updeted version.
